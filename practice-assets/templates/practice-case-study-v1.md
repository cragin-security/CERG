## Situation

What was the client's state before engagement? 2-3 sentences describing the pain points that drove the engagement. Focus on structural patterns, not the client identity.

**Example:** "A mid-market SaaS company with 8 security FTEs had a policy document that had never been signed, an ad hoc risk process driven by audit requests, and recurring SOC 2 findings because evidence was assembled annually rather than produced as a byproduct of operations."

## Solution

What did the practice deliver? 3-5 bullet points:

- Organizational profile built, corpus rendered and adapted
- MVC spine adopted with CISO and Executive Sponsor sign-off
- [Overlay package(s)] selected and implemented
- [Specific CERG artifacts] activated and producing evidence

## Outcome

What changed? Quantifiable results:

- Risk register: from 0 entries to [#] with [#] review cycles completed
- Policy signed within [days] of engagement start
- Maturity moved from [initial] to [final]
- [Regulatory-specific outcome, e.g., "PCI SAQ D evidence package assembled ahead of QSAR"]
- [Client metric, e.g., "Audit prep time reduced from 6 weeks to 5 days"]

## What Made This Engagement Work

2-3 specific decisions or assets that were most impactful:

- Specific sequencing decision that was correct
- Specific practice asset that saved time
- Specific client adaptation that made the difference

## What We'd Do Differently

1-2 things the practice would change for a similar engagement next time:

- Sequencing change
- Asset that should have been created or updated
- Client readiness indicator that should have been checked earlier

## Key Takeaway for Practice

One sentence summarizing what this engagement taught the practice about this sector, tier, or overlay.

## 4. Client Responsibilities

Client agrees to:
1. Name an Executive Sponsor who will endorse the Cybersecurity Policy
2. Name a CISO (or equivalent) as the primary point of contact
3. Provide timely access to systems, personnel, and information needed for the engagement
4. Review and provide feedback on adapted artifacts within 5 business days of delivery
5. Participate in weekly check-ins and the first risk register review
6. Comply with Consultant's reasonable security and confidentiality requirements for access to client systems

## 5. Consultant Responsibilities

Consultant agrees to:
1. Perform the services with professional care and in accordance with the scope
2. Provide a named Engagement Lead and escalation path to the Practice Principal
3. Protect client data per the Data Handling and Confidentiality Standards (Exhibit B)
4. Deliver the artifacts listed in the Engagement Scope (Exhibit A)
5. Not share client-specific information outside the engagement without written consent

## 6. Intellectual Property

**Consultant IP:** Consultant retains ownership of all pre-existing intellectual property, including the CERG framework, practice templates, tools, and methodologies.

**Client IP:** Client retains ownership of the adapted CERG corpus rendered for their organization, their organization profile file, their risk register data, and any client-created artifacts.

**Feedback:** Client feedback and suggestions about the CERG framework may be used by Consultant to improve the framework, provided no client-confidential information is disclosed.

## 7. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to protect confidential information disclosed during the engagement. Consultant's confidentiality obligations survive the engagement for 2 years. Client's confidentiality obligations (regarding Consultant's proprietary materials) survive indefinitely.

## 8. Limitation of Liability

Consultant's aggregate liability for all claims arising from this engagement is limited to the total fees paid for this Statement of Work. Consultant is not liable for:
- Client's regulatory certification outcome (CERG is a framework, not a guarantee of compliance)
- Damages arising from client's failure to implement recommended controls
- Third-party service provider actions

## 9. Independent Contractor

Consultant is an independent contractor, not an employee or agent of Client. Consultant controls the manner and method of services delivery.

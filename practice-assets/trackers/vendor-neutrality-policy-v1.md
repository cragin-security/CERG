# Independence and Vendor-Neutrality Policy v1
## Practice Asset — Not a CERP Corpus Document

---

## Policy Statement

The CERG consulting practice is technology-agnostic and vendor-independent. We do not sell, resell, endorse, or receive commissions from security tool vendors. Our recommendations are based on the client's requirements, environment, and budget — not on vendor relationships.

## What We Do

- **Recommend capability categories** — e.g., "your CDE needs an ASV scanning vendor; categories to evaluate include Approved Scanning Vendor qualification, scan frequency, and reporting format"
- **Provide evaluation criteria** — what to look for in a solution for a given control gap
- **Review vendor selections** — assess whether a chosen vendor meets the control requirements
- **Integrate with existing tools** — CERG is tool-agnostic; we adapt to whatever the client already has

## What We Do Not Do

| Activity | Prohibited | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Resell or broker tool licenses | Yes | Creates conflict of interest; compromises independence |
| Accept vendor commissions, referral fees, or kickbacks | Yes | Violates fiduciary duty to client |
| Recommend a specific vendor by name in advisory deliverables | Yes* | *May name evaluated vendors in assessment findings if the client explicitly requests competitive analysis, but the recommendation must include at least 3 alternatives |
| Write RFPs or procurement specifications that favor a specific vendor | Yes | Client procurement should be independent |
| Serve as a vendor's authorized partner | Yes | Incompatible with advisory role |

## Exceptions

Exceptions require written approval from the Practice Principal and disclosure to the client:

1. **Toolchain for engagement delivery.** The practice may use standard tooling (GitHub, project management, communication platforms) to deliver services. These are practice operations tools, not client security tool recommendations.
2. **Compliance requirement.** If a regulatory requirement (e.g., PCI DSS ASV scan) mandates a specific vendor class (Approved Scanning Vendor), the practice explains the requirement and provides the vendor list — but does not select the vendor.
3. **Client request for vendor recommendation.** If a client explicitly requests a vendor recommendation (not just a capability category), the practice provides a written evaluation of at least 3 vendors with assessment criteria and leaves the decision to the client.

## Disclosure Requirements

The practice maintains a register of any relationships with security vendors (former employment, advisory board seats, stock ownership). This register is disclosed to any client who requests it. No practitioner with a current vendor relationship may advise a client on selecting that vendor.

## Enforcement

Violations of this policy are grounds for termination of the practitioner's engagement with the practice. Clients who suspect a violation should contact the Practice Principal directly.

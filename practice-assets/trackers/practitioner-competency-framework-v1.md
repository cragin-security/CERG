# Practitioner Competency Framework v1
## Practice Asset — Not a CERP Corpus Document

---

## Purpose

This framework defines the knowledge, skills, and experience required to deliver CERG consulting engagements at each tier. It ensures every client receives a practitioner who is competent in the CERG corpus, the consulting model, and the client's sector.

---

## Competency Levels

| Level | Tier Authorization | Experience | Can Lead |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate** | Tier 1 (supervised) | < 6 months practice experience or < 3 completed CERG engagements | Tier 1 with senior review |
| **Practitioner** | Tier 1, Tier 2 | 6-18 months practice experience or 3-8 CERG engagements | Tier 1 independently, Tier 2 with review |
| **Senior Practitioner** | Tier 1-3 | 18+ months practice experience or 8+ CERG engagements | Tier 1-2 independently, Tier 3 with principal review |
| **Principal** | All tiers | 3+ years practice experience or practice founder | All tiers independently; practice methodology authority |

## Certification Path

| Level | Requirements | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Associate | Complete practitioner onboarding guide (2 weeks) | Onboarding checklist sign-off by Senior or Principal |
| Practitioner | Lead 1 Tier 1 engagement independently; lead 1 Tier 2 engagement with review | Engagement debrief signed by Practice Principal |
| Senior | Lead 3 Tier 2 engagements independently; lead 1 Tier 3 engagement with review | Engagement outcomes meet success criteria; at least 1 upstream contribution filed |
| Principal | 3+ years practice experience; demonstrated methodology contribution | Practice Principal designation by firm leadership |

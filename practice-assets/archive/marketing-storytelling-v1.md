## The Core Narrative

Most security programs fail not because of bad tools or bad people — but because of bad structure. Cybersecurity operations are fragmented: engineering builds controls, compliance produces binders, risk manages spreadsheets, and no one connects the three. The CISO spends audit season reconstructing evidence that should have been produced as a byproduct of normal work.

CERG replaces the fragmented model with a unified operating model. One control baseline. One evidence library. One risk methodology. Three pillars that work together, not in parallel.

The consulting practice accelerates this transition. Clients get a running program in 30 days, not 18 months. They get a signed policy, a populated risk register, and a cadence that produces evidence without manual assembly.

## Sector One-Pagers

### Technology / SaaS
**Hook:** "Your security program should scale as fast as your product."
**Problem:** SOC 2 findings recur because evidence is assembled annually rather than produced continuously. Customer security reviews consume engineering time.
**CERG solution:** Evidence produced by cadence, not by calendar. Risk register runs on day 30, not month 12. Customer-facing security posture becomes a competitive advantage, not a bottleneck.

### Financial Services
**Hook:** "SOX is not a separate compliance program; it is a scope filter over controls you already operate."
**Problem:** SOX ITGC and PCI audits run on parallel tracks with duplicate evidence. External auditors find control gaps because evidence is collected for one framework but not mapped to the other.
**CERG solution:** One control baseline, one evidence library, one operating cadence. SOX and PCI evidence are produced simultaneously from the same procedures.

### Healthcare / Life Sciences
**Hook:** "HIPAA compliance starts with what you already have: a functioning security program, not a binder of policies."
**Problem:** PHI breach risk drives regulatory action. Business associate agreements are managed outside the security program. Clinical trial CUI adds another overlay.
**CERG solution:** HIPAA Security Rule maps to existing controls via the CUI overlay. BA agreements flow through the TPRM process. Privacy Rule requirements live in the privacy operational package.

### Manufacturing / OT
**Hook:** "Your factory floor runs on control systems. Your security program should too."
**Problem:** IT security practices break OT. Patching windows, scanning methods, and change controls designed for servers don't work on PLCs. The plant floor is a blind spot until ransomware finds it.
**CERG solution:** OT-specific controls (passive scanning, zone segmentation, extended SLAs) layer onto the same operating model that governs IT. No parallel universes.

### Energy / Utilities
**Hook:** "NERC-CIP compliance and cybersecurity maturity are not the same thing. You need both."
**Problem:** CIP compliance is strong but detection engineering is weak. BES Cyber Systems are protected per regulation, but the IT side and non-BES OT are underinvested.
**CERG solution:** NERC-CIP overlay ensures compliance. The core CERG model adds detection, exposure management, and architecture review that CIP does not require but security needs.

### Defense Industrial Base
**Hook:** "CMMC certification is an outcome, not a program. The program is what comes before the assessment."
**Problem:** CUI scope is too broad, POA&Ms are too old, and SSP evidence is assembled the week before the C3PAO arrives.
**CERG solution:** CUI scope is defined upfront. POA&Ms are tracked with owners and dates. Evidence is produced quarterly. The assessment is a check, not a surprise.

### Government / Federal
**Hook:** "Federal security is 800-53. So is CERG. The adaptation is in the reporting, not the controls."
**Problem:** FISMA reporting, ATO renewals, and OIG audits run on different cycles with different evidence definitions. The same control is tested three times with three different evidence requests.
**CERG solution:** One NIST 800-53 baseline, one evidence library, crosswalked to every federal reporting requirement.

### Cross-sector / Holding
**Hook:** "Your subsidiaries have different regulators. Your security model should not."
**Problem:** Each subsidiary runs its own security program with different policies, different risk tolerances, and different audit cycles. The holding company has no consolidated view.
**CERG solution:** Central CERG corpus with per-subsidiary rendering. Shared evidence model. Consolidated risk reporting. One operating model, multiple scope filters.
